# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the
Gravity community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

Gravity is built to welcome both first-time creators who have never written code
and seasoned engineers. We pledge to act and interact in ways that keep that
door open to everyone.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Being kind, welcoming, and patient — especially with newcomers asking
  "obvious" questions.
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments, public or private.
- Trolling, deliberately dismissive, or demeaning responses to questions.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Any other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a
  professional setting.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the project maintainers at **joey@eet.nu**. All complaints will be
reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to
respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may
face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by the project's
leadership.
